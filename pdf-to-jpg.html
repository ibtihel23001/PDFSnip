FAQ

PDF to JPG Questions

Is my PDF file uploaded to a server? +
No. PDFSnip's PDF to JPG tool runs 100% in your browser using PDF.js — an open-source PDF renderer by Mozilla. Your file never leaves your device. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.
What DPI should I choose? +
For screen/email use, 1× (72 DPI) is fine. For presentations and sharing, 2× (150 DPI) is the sweet spot. For printing or archiving, use 3× (300 DPI) for crystal-clear output.
Can I convert a multi-page PDF? +
Yes! Every page becomes a separate JPG. Download them individually or use "Download All as ZIP" to get them bundled in one click.
Why is conversion slow for large PDFs? +
Since all processing happens in your browser (not a server), conversion speed depends on your device's CPU. Large PDFs with many pages or complex graphics may take a few seconds per page.
What if my PDF has a password? +
Password-protected PDFs are not supported in the current version. Please remove the password from your PDF first using Adobe Acrobat or another tool, then convert here.
