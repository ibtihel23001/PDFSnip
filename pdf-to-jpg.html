FAQ

PDF to JPG Questions

What resolution should I choose for PDF to JPG conversion? +
For screen viewing and email, 72–96 DPI is sufficient. For presentations, 150 DPI works well. For printing or archiving, use 300 DPI for the sharpest results. Our default of 150 DPI is a good balance of quality and file size.
Can I convert a multi-page PDF to JPG? +
Yes! Each page in your PDF becomes a separate JPG image. You can download them individually or as a ZIP archive containing all pages in order.
Will the image quality be good enough for printing? +
Absolutely. Select "High (300 DPI)" in the settings and set JPG quality to 95–100% for print-ready output. This produces professional-grade images suitable for physical printing.
Is there a limit on how many pages can be converted? +
There is no page limit. You can convert PDFs with hundreds of pages. Conversion time scales linearly — a 100-page document typically converts in under 30 seconds.
Why would I convert PDF to JPG instead of PNG? +
JPG files are smaller and more compatible for web use, social media, and email. PNG is better when you need transparency or lossless quality. We also offer a PDF to PNG tool if that's what you need.
Does converting PDF to JPG remove my personal data? +
The conversion process renders your PDF visually into an image — any embedded text, metadata, or form data in the PDF will not be present in the JPG output. The image only contains the visual content of the page.
